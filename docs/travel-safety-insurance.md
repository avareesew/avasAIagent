# Travel Safety & Insurance Guide

## Why Travel Insurance Is Non-Negotiable

Medical evacuation alone can cost $50,000-$200,000+ without insurance. A comprehensive travel insurance policy costs $50-$150 for a typical 2-week trip — one of the best value purchases in travel.

## What Good Travel Insurance Covers

### Medical Coverage
- Emergency medical treatment abroad
- Medical evacuation to nearest adequate facility
- Repatriation (flying home if medically necessary)
- Minimum recommended coverage: $100,000 medical, $500,000 evacuation

### Trip Coverage
- Trip cancellation (illness, death in family, natural disaster)
- Trip interruption (cutting trip short)
- Travel delay (missed connections, weather)
- Baggage loss or delay

### Activity Coverage
- Standard policies exclude "adventure sports"
- If hiking, skiing, scuba diving, motorcycling — verify these are covered
- Add adventure sports rider if needed

## Destination Safety Tips

### General Safety
- Register with your country's embassy before international travel (free, takes 2 minutes)
- Keep digital and physical copies of your passport separate from the originals
- Share your itinerary with someone at home
- Be aware of common scams in your destination (research before you go)
- Use hotel safes for passports and extra cash

### Transportation Safety
- Use licensed taxis or app-based rideshare (Grab, Uber, Bolt) — never unmarked cabs
- Verify bus and train companies have good safety records before overnight journeys
- Wear helmets on motorbikes — even if locals don't
- Don't travel by bus at night in regions with poor road safety records (parts of South/Southeast Asia, Central America)

### Health Precautions
- Check CDC traveler health notices for your destination: cdc.gov/travel
- Recommended vaccinations vary by region — consult a travel medicine clinic 4-8 weeks before departure
- Common travel vaccines: Hepatitis A, Typhoid, Yellow Fever (required for some countries)
- Drink bottled or purified water in countries with water quality concerns
- Use insect repellent with DEET in malaria/dengue regions

### Common Travel Scams to Avoid
- "My friend's shop is cheaper" — taxi/tuk-tuk drivers get commission to redirect you
- Fake police officers asking to inspect your wallet
- Distraction theft in crowded tourist areas
- Overpriced currency exchange — always check the actual exchange rate first
- Gem investment scams (common in Southeast Asia)

## Emergency Contacts

Before traveling, write down:
1. Local emergency number (not always 911 — research per country)
2. Your country's embassy phone number in each destination
3. Your insurance provider's 24/7 emergency line
4. Your bank's international collect call number

## What To Do If Something Goes Wrong

**Lost passport:** Contact your country's nearest embassy immediately. Most can issue emergency travel documents within 24-48 hours.

**Medical emergency:** Go to the nearest hospital. Call your insurance's emergency line from the hospital so they can arrange direct payment or guide you to the best facility.

**Theft:** File a police report within 24 hours (required for insurance claims). Block your cards immediately via your bank's app.
