# Travel Budget Framework

## The Four Budget Categories

Every travel budget breaks down into four core categories:

1. **Flights** — typically 30-40% of total trip cost. Book 6-8 weeks out for domestic, 3-6 months for international. Use Tuesday/Wednesday departures for lower fares. Avoid booking in peak season windows (holiday weekends, spring break).

2. **Accommodation** — typically 25-35% of budget. Options by cost tier:
   - Hostels/dorms: $15-$40/night
   - Budget hotels/guesthouses: $40-$80/night
   - Mid-range hotels: $80-$150/night
   - Upscale hotels: $150+/night
   Staying slightly outside city centers can cut costs 30-50%.

3. **Food** — typically 15-25% of budget. Daily food costs by style:
   - Street food/markets: $10-$20/day
   - Mix of casual restaurants: $30-$50/day
   - Sit-down restaurants: $50-$80/day
   Cooking at accommodation saves significant money on longer trips.

4. **Activities & Transport** — typically 15-25% of budget. Includes local transit, tours, entrance fees, and day trips.

## Emergency Fund Rule

Always budget 10-15% on top of your estimated total as an emergency buffer. This covers unexpected costs: medical expenses, rebooking fees, lost items, or simply overspending on food and activities.

## Budget Calculation Formula

```
Total Trip Budget = (Daily Budget × Number of Days) + Flights + (Emergency Buffer × 1.15)
```

Example: 10-day trip to Japan, mid-range
- Daily budget: $120/day × 10 = $1,200
- Flights: $900
- Buffer (15%): $315
- Total: ~$2,415
