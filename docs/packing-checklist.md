# Travel Packing & Logistics Checklist

## The Golden Rule: Pack Light

Carry-on only travel saves money (no checked bag fees), time (no baggage claim), and energy. A 20-40L backpack or carry-on bag is sufficient for trips up to 2 weeks.

## Documents Checklist

- [ ] Passport (valid for 6+ months beyond travel dates)
- [ ] Visa(s) — research requirements for every country you're visiting
- [ ] Travel insurance documents (policy number + emergency phone number)
- [ ] Copies of passport stored separately from the original
- [ ] Digital copies in cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox)
- [ ] Vaccination records if required (yellow fever, etc.)
- [ ] Travel itinerary with hotel addresses (for customs forms)
- [ ] Emergency contacts list (printed and digital)

## Clothing — Carry-On Strategy

Pack the minimum and plan to do laundry every 5-7 days. For a 1-2 week trip:
- 3-4 shirts (quick-dry material recommended)
- 2 pairs of pants/shorts
- 1 nicer outfit for restaurants or events
- 5-7 pairs of underwear and socks
- 1 lightweight jacket or fleece
- 1 rain jacket (packs small, used constantly)
- Comfortable walking shoes + flip flops or sandals

## Tech & Electronics

- [ ] Phone + charger
- [ ] Universal power adapter (essential for international travel)
- [ ] Portable battery pack (10,000mAh minimum)
- [ ] Earbuds/headphones
- [ ] Camera if desired (phone cameras are excellent for most travelers)
- [ ] Laptop or tablet if working remotely

## Health & Safety

- [ ] Prescription medications (2-week supply + extra)
- [ ] Basic first aid: ibuprofen, antihistamines, antidiarrheal, bandages
- [ ] Sunscreen
- [ ] Insect repellent (for tropical destinations)
- [ ] Water purification tablets or filter (for backpacking or remote areas)
- [ ] Hand sanitizer

## Money & Finances

- [ ] Notify bank of travel dates before departure
- [ ] Bring two cards from different networks (Visa + Mastercard)
- [ ] Keep $100-$200 USD in cash as emergency backup
- [ ] Research local ATM fees — use bank-affiliated ATMs when possible
- [ ] Know the local currency and rough exchange rate

## Pre-Departure Logistics

- [ ] Book or confirm accommodation for first 2-3 nights
- [ ] Research transportation from airport to city center
- [ ] Download offline maps (Google Maps, Maps.me) for your destinations
- [ ] Download translation app (Google Translate) with offline language packs
- [ ] Check travel advisories for your destination (state.gov for US travelers)
- [ ] Set up international phone plan or plan to use local SIMs

## At the Airport

- Arrive 2.5-3 hours early for international flights
- Keep carry-on under 7-10kg (most airlines) or 10kg (US domestic)
- Liquids rule: containers max 100ml, all in 1-quart clear bag
- Wear your heaviest shoes and jacket through security to save bag weight

## On Arrival

- Get local currency from ATM at or near airport (not exchange kiosks — poor rates)
- Buy a local SIM card if staying more than a few days ($5-$15 for data)
- Take a screenshot or download your accommodation address before leaving airport WiFi
