# Southeast Asia Budget Travel Guide

## Overview

Southeast Asia is one of the world's best budget travel destinations. Countries like Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia, Indonesia, and the Philippines offer rich culture, stunning scenery, and excellent food at very low cost.

## Country-by-Country Daily Budget

### Thailand
- Budget: $30-$50/day
- Mid-range: $60-$100/day
- Best for: beaches, temples, street food, nightlife
- Food costs: Street food $1-$3/meal, restaurants $5-$15/meal
- Accommodation: Hostels $8-$15, budget hotels $20-$40
- Best months to visit: November-February (cool and dry)
- Avoid: April (hottest month), monsoon season June-October

### Vietnam
- Budget: $25-$40/day
- Mid-range: $50-$80/day
- Best for: history, cuisine, motorbike travel, Ha Long Bay
- Food costs: Pho $1-$2, local meals $2-$5, restaurants $5-$15
- Accommodation: Hostels $6-$12, guesthouses $15-$30
- Best months to visit: Feb-April (central/south), Oct-Dec (north)

### Cambodia
- Budget: $20-$35/day
- Mid-range: $40-$70/day
- Best for: Angkor Wat, river life, low crowds
- Angkor Wat entrance: $37/day, $62/3-day pass
- Food costs: Local meals $2-$4, tourist restaurants $6-$12

### Indonesia (Bali)
- Budget: $30-$50/day
- Mid-range: $60-$100/day
- Best for: surfing, temples, rice terraces, wellness
- Food costs: Warungs (local cafes) $2-$5, restaurants $8-$20
- Accommodation: Guesthouses $15-$30, mid-range villas $60-$100

### Philippines
- Budget: $25-$45/day
- Mid-range: $50-$90/day
- Best for: islands, diving, beaches
- Inter-island flights are affordable ($20-$60)

## Transportation Tips

- **Overnight buses/trains** save accommodation costs and travel time simultaneously
- **Grab app** (like Uber) works across most of Southeast Asia and is cheaper than taxis
- **Motorbike rentals** cost $5-$10/day and offer maximum flexibility
- **Ferries** between islands are scenic and cheap ($5-$30)

## Visa Information

- Thailand: 30-day visa-free for most Western passports
- Vietnam: E-visa required ($25), valid 90 days
- Cambodia: Visa on arrival or e-visa ($30)
- Indonesia: 30-day visa-free for most Western passports
- Philippines: 30-day visa-free for most Western passports

## Money-Saving Tips

1. Eat where locals eat — street stalls and markets are safer and cheaper than tourist restaurants
2. Negotiate tuk-tuk and taxi prices before riding
3. Book accommodation day-by-day for flexibility, except during high season
4. Carry small bills — many vendors don't have change
5. Use ATMs sparingly — withdrawal fees add up; withdraw larger amounts less frequently
