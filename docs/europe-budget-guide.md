# Europe Budget Travel Guide

## Overview

Europe spans a wide range of costs. Western Europe (UK, France, Switzerland, Scandinavia) is expensive; Eastern Europe (Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria) is among the best-value travel in the world. Budget carefully based on your specific route.

## Daily Budget by Region

### Western Europe
- Budget: $80-$100/day
- Mid-range: $120-$180/day
- Countries: France, Germany, Netherlands, Spain, Italy, UK, Austria, Belgium
- Tip: Spain and Portugal are significantly cheaper than France and UK

### Eastern Europe
- Budget: $35-$55/day
- Mid-range: $60-$100/day
- Countries: Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria, Croatia
- Prague, Krakow, Budapest are outstanding value — half the cost of Paris or Amsterdam

### Scandinavia
- Budget: $100-$130/day
- Mid-range: $160-$220/day
- Countries: Norway, Sweden, Denmark, Finland
- One of the most expensive regions in the world to travel

## Accommodation Costs

| Type | Western Europe | Eastern Europe |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Hostel dorm | $25-$45 | $10-$20 |
| Budget hotel | $70-$120 | $30-$60 |
| Mid-range hotel | $120-$200 | $60-$100 |
| Airbnb (private room) | $60-$110 | $25-$55 |

## Transportation

### Budget Airlines
- Ryanair, EasyJet, Wizz Air fly across Europe for $20-$80 one-way
- Book 4-8 weeks in advance for best prices
- Watch out for baggage fees — budget airlines charge $15-$30 for checked bags

### Trains
- Eurail Pass is rarely cost-effective for short trips
- Point-to-point tickets on Trainline or national rail websites are usually cheaper
- Overnight trains save a night of accommodation

### Buses
- FlixBus connects 100+ European cities for $5-$25
- Slowest option but cheapest, especially for Eastern Europe

## Food Costs

- Street food/markets: $5-$15/meal (Western), $3-$8/meal (Eastern)
- Casual restaurants: $12-$25/meal (Western), $6-$14/meal (Eastern)
- Supermarkets: buy breakfast and snacks to cut food budget by 30-40%
- Lunch is the main meal in Southern Europe — set lunch menus (€10-€15) often include multiple courses

## Free Activities in Major Cities

- **Paris**: Louvre (free first Sunday of month), Musée d'Orsay (free first Sunday), Sacré-Cœur, street art in Belleville
- **Rome**: Vatican exterior, Trevi Fountain, Pantheon (exterior), Colosseum area
- **Berlin**: East Side Gallery, Memorial to the Murdered Jews of Europe, Tiergarten
- **Prague**: Old Town Square, Charles Bridge, Vyšehrad, free walking tours
- **Amsterdam**: Vondelpark, Jordaan neighborhood, free ferry across IJ waterway

## Money-Saving Tips

1. Travel in shoulder season (April-May or September-October) for 20-30% savings on flights and accommodation
2. Buy a city transport day pass — almost always cheaper than individual tickets
3. Cook at least one meal per day if staying in accommodation with a kitchen
4. Book popular museums in advance online — skip-the-line tickets often cost the same
5. Free walking tours operate in almost every major European city (tip-based)
